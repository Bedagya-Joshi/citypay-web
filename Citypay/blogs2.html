How To Receive Remittance Digitally from Abroad Using City pay Wallet

Remittances serve as a vital lifeline for many households in Nepal, providing essential financial support and significantly influencing the country's economy. A recent study using a Keynesian framework found that remittances positively impact Nepal’s economic resilience. Over the years, the inflow of remittances has increased significantly, leading to growth in key macroeconomic variables such as Gross National Product (GNP), private consumption, government expenditure, and imports. For every one-unit increase in remittances, there is a corresponding increase in consumption (0.89 units), imports (1.25 units), investment (0.32 units), and GNP (2.14 units). These inflows contribute to overall economic stability and development. In the fiscal year 2022/23, an astonishing 771,000 young individuals embarked on foreign employment from Nepal, according to the Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE). This figure includes both new and re-permit workers who acquired work permits during the fiscal year 2022/2023. During Shrawan 2023 (mid-July to mid-August), Nepali migrant workers sent a historic high of Rs. 116.2 billion in remittances. In the fiscal year 2021/22, remittance inflows increased to Rs. 1,007.31 billion, accounting for 20.8% of Nepal’s GDP.


The Evolution of Remittance Method: From Traditional to Digital

In the past, migrant workers faced significant challenges when sending money to their families in Nepal. Traditional methods, such as bank transfers and wire transfers, were not only slow and costly but also required extensive paperwork. However, technological advancements have revolutionized the remittance process. Digital platforms, mobile apps, and blockchain technology now offer faster, more secure options. Furthermore, artificial intelligence enhances security and personalization. As a result, digital remittance is gradually replacing outdated approaches, providing a convenient and efficient solution for international money transfers.

How to Receive Remittance Using City Pay Wallet

With the advancement in technology, receiving remittances has become more accessible and convenient. Migrant workers can easily send their money directly to a City Pay wallet in Nepal. City Pay is Nepal’s first online payment gateway that allows users to make online transactions, pay bills, and receive money. It has made receiving remittances from abroad more accessible, convenient, and faster. Receiving remittances through City Pay is a simple and hassle-free process. Follow these steps to receive remittance digitally from abroad using City Pay:


Step 1: Register for a City Pay account


The first step to receiving remittance through City Pay is to register for a City Pay account. To register, you will need to provide your name, mobile number, and email address, and create a password. You will also need to verify your mobile number through a One Time Password (OTP) sent to your phone.


Step 2: Provide your KYC details


To comply with the regulations of the Nepal Rastra Bank, City Pay requires users to complete their Know Your Customer (KYC) details. You can complete your KYC details by visiting a City Pay KYC section /center and providing your identity proof, such as your citizenship certificate or passport.


Step 3: Receive remittance


Once you have completed the above steps, you are now ready to receive remittance through City Pay. To receive a remittance, you will need to provide the following information to the sender:
- Your name as it appears on your City Pay account
- Your address information
- Your mobile number registered with City Pay
The sender can then initiate the remittance transfer using their preferred remittance service provider, such as WorldRemit, Remitly, MoneyGram, etc. The remittance amount will then be transferred to your City Pay account.


Step 4: Withdraw the remittance


Once the remittance has been transferred to your City Pay account, you can start using your funds for making payments of bills. In addition to this, you can also transfer the funds to desired bank accounts.
